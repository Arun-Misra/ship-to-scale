## 2. Competitive Landscape

### 2.1 The Fundamental Problem: Text-to-SQL Alone Fails

Before competitor details, this context is essential: raw AI text-to-SQL (having an LLM write SQL on the fly) is fundamentally unreliable for business decisions at production scale. This is why Viriya's semantic brain is an architectural necessity, not a feature.

- On **LiveSQLBench-Large** (complex schemas, ~1,000 columns, 54 tables): popular LLMs achieve only **30–36% accuracy** [(dbt Developer Blog 2026)](https://docs.getdbt.com/blog/semantic-layer-vs-text-to-sql-2026)
- Top performer on Spider 2.0-Lite: **69.65%** — a curated benchmark, real-world is lower [(dbt Developer Blog 2026)](https://docs.getdbt.com/blog/semantic-layer-vs-text-to-sql-2026)
- **81.2% of text-to-SQL failures** are schema-level errors: wrong column selection and semantic misinterpretation [(Omni Analytics)](https://omni.co/blog/why-text-to-sql-fails)
- Production accuracy for open-ended questions against real enterprise systems: **10–20%** for business-decision-quality answers [(Promethium research)](https://promethium.ai/guides/enterprise-text-to-sql-accuracy-benchmarks-2/)

> *"With a semantic layer, failure looks like an error message. Text-to-SQL will cheerfully give you a wrong number — for anything going to a board deck, an auditor, or a company KPI dashboard, that difference is everything."* — [dbt Developer Blog, 2026](https://docs.getdbt.com/blog/semantic-layer-vs-text-to-sql-2026)

The three structural failure modes [(Omni Analytics)](https://omni.co/blog/why-text-to-sql-fails):
1. **Metric misinterpretation** — "FRT" parsed as "First Resolution Time" vs. "First Response Time"; executes without error, looks right, is wrong
2. **Non-determinism** — same question produces different SQL on different runs → different numbers → invisible drift over time
3. **Incorrect join paths** — plausible but wrong join sequences; LLM silently produces incorrect aggregations with no error

The semantic layer [(Hacker News community)](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=43398507) [(VentureBeat)](https://venturebeat.com/ai/headless-vs-native-semantic-layer-the-architectural-key-to-unlocking-90-text) [(Holistics)](https://www.holistics.io/blog/text-to-sql/) is the solution the industry has converged on — and Viriya's per-tenant semantic brain is its implementation of this.

### 4.4 Moat Analysis

**Moat 1 — Per-Tenant Semantic Brain (Primary)**
Every definition captured through use (revenue = X at Company A, revenue = Y at Company B) is stored per-tenant in Viriya's infrastructure, not the customer's. The definitions are non-portable. Switching away from Viriya means starting from scratch: re-training a new tool on every business definition, relearning every edge case, rebuilding every semantic nuance accumulated over months. The moat deepens with every question answered.

**Moat 2 — Integration Depth**
- Owns the recurring reports (weekly, monthly board decks)
- Lives in Slack (the daily workflow — no login friction for day-to-day use)
- dbt manifest import on day one inherits the company's existing metric work
- Removing Viriya is not "cancel a subscription" — it's "re-hire an analyst and rebuild all reports"

**Moat 3 — Enterprise Trust Gauntlet**
The second moat for any serious competitor: SOC2, SSO, audit logs, row-level security, custom data retention policies. This takes **12–18 months** to clear even for well-funded startups. Viriya builds this on the growth path; copycat tools must queue for the same gauntlet.

**Moat 4 — Read-Only Safety as Trust Foundation**
The product's architectural commitment to never mutating source data is a trust-building mechanism with no shortcut. Competitors that add write capabilities to expand features trade away this trust asymmetrically. Viriya's read-only guarantee is a structural moat against feature-creep competitors.

The independent AI-native BI market is consolidating, but the winner hasn't emerged. These exits validate acquisition interest while reducing direct competition.

2. **ThoughtSpot validated the autonomous investigation agent** with Spotter 3 (late 2025) [(ThoughtSpot)](https://www.thoughtspot.com/press-releases/thoughtspot-launches-four-bi-agents-that-work-as-a-team-to-deliver-modern-analytics). The enterprise market now has a reference case for what agentic analytics looks like. Viriya offers the same capability to startups at 1/100th the **$140K ACV** [(Vendr)](https://www.vendr.com/marketplace/thoughtspot).

3. **AI analytics is growing 3x faster than traditional BI.** Traditional BI: **9% CAGR** [(Grand View Research)](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/business-intelligence-software-market). AI analytics sub-market: **29% CAGR** [(Precedence Research)](https://www.precedenceresearch.com/ai-in-data-analytics-market). Building Viriya now is the right timing.

4. **The analyst talent shortage is structural.** **76% of organizations** can't find data talent [(Business.com)](https://www.business.com/articles/big-data-big-problem-coping-with-shortage-of-talent-in-data-analysis/), **51-day** average hire time [(JADA Squad)](https://www.jadasquad.com/blogs/how-to-hire-a-data-analyst-2025-guide), salary floors rising year over year. The problem Viriya solves is getting worse, not better.

### Positioning Recommendations

**Lead with the "why" investigation.** Every competitor can answer "what is revenue this week?" Viriya's differentiated capability — autonomous root-cause investigation with streamed reasoning — is the demo moment no competitor can replicate.

**Own the "first data employee" framing.** At $199/month vs. **$129,000/year fully loaded for an analyst** [(Glassdoor)](https://www.glassdoor.com/Salaries/us-data-analyst-salary-SRCH_IL.0,2_IN1_KO3,15.htm) [(Vena)](https://www.venasolutions.com/blog/how-much-does-an-employee-cost), the positioning is self-evident for any founder who has tried to hire data talent.

**Target the "Tableau refugee" segment.** Tableau's BI mindshare dropped **61% year-over-year** [(GeekWire)](https://www.geekwire.com/2024/an-inside-look-at-tableau-whats-the-future-of-the-datafam-at-salesforce/). Its customers are actively looking for alternatives. Any startup that received a Tableau quote over $20,000 and walked away is a warm Viriya prospect.

**Slack virality is the distribution moat, not just a feature.** Frame it not as "we have a bot" but as "Viriya spreads through your organization the same way Slack did — through daily use visibility, not IT procurement."
